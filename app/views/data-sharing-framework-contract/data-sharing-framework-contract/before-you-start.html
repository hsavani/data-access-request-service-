Why you need a Data Sharing Framework Contract

Summary

Any organisation that requests access to data from NHS Digital must have an active Data Sharing Framework Contract (DSFC).

You can use this online service to apply for a DSFC. You’ll also need a Data Sharing Agreement (DSA) which sets out what data you are requesting and why you are requesting it.

NHS Digital's statutory function

NHS Digital collects, analyses, publishes and shares some of its health and social care data. On occasions, data is distributed, and other people are permitted to use it.

Your DSFC

The DSFC creates a framework of legally binding terms and conditions. The terms and conditions apply each time NHS Digital agrees to share data with a recipient.

NHS Digital will not share data with any organisation that does not hold a DSFC unless the organisation is a Processor acting on behalf of the data receiver, and NHS Digital has agreed to share the data with that Processor.

Where datasets include Personal Data, the organisation requesting the data becomes a Controller of the Personal Data as part of their DCFC.

Your DSA

A DSA does not guarantee that NHS Digital will agree to share data with the requesting organisation.

The sharing of data by NHS Digital is at their discretion and subject to NHS Digital’s current terms and conditions.

The DSA sets out terms and conditions that help organisation’s understand what is permitted each time they receive and use data for a particular purpose.

Before you start

Ensure you have:

  • your organisations registered name and address as it appears in the Information Commissioners Office (ICO) Data Protection Register
  • the name of the person who will sign the DSFC on behalf of your organisation
