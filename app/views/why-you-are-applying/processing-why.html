Standard Description:

An application for data shall clearly explain the following:

  1. What is the aim and purpose of this application?
    1. A brief explanation of the Article 6 justification of processing the data (e.g. legitimate interest). If multiple sections of Article 6 are being used, then it should be made clear which purposes are covered under which section.
    2. A brief explanation of the public interest (for processing under Article 9(2)(i) and (j)), or substantial public interest (if processing under Article 9(2)(g)).
      1. Are there any moral or ethical issues raised by the proposed dissemination? What is the risk of potential harm to the public by the dissemination?
      2. And where the processing meets the requirement in point (g) of Article 9(2) of the GDPR for a basis in the law of the United Kingdom or a part of the United Kingdom it meets a condition in Part 2 of Schedule 1 of the Data Protection Act 2018
  2. How will the data requested achieve the aim identified?
  3. Any relevant background to the request e.g. a longitudinal research study or a funded programme of work which commenced in XX year.
  4. Clearly explain the relationship between the proposed project under this application and any wider project, collaboration, associated work or follow up.
  5. Clearly explain the relationship between the proposed project under this application and any wider project, collaboration, associated work or follow up.
    1. The scope of data use should be clearly identified e.g. where the data is used in a project which is a component of a larger programme or where the project consists of multiple phases the application should clearly identify the scope of the data use.
  6. Clearly describe the data subjects, including e.g. control and cohort groups.
  7. The purpose of the request:
    1. Provide detailed information about the purpose of the project under this application.
    2. What data is required?
    3. Why is the data required for the purpose? This section should include justification for:
      1. The datasets requested
      2. The level of data (identifying, pseudonymised, unsuppressed aggregated)
      3. Justification for the number of years requested
      4. Justification for the geographical spread of the data requested
      5. Confirm there are no alternative, less intrusive ways of achieving the purpose
    4. Details of any efforts taken to minimise the data required
  8. What organisations are involved including:
    1. Who is the Data Controller (sole, joint) and who is the Data Processor?
    2. Are there any organisations which are involved in the wider project (but not processing the data) and if so the nature of their role?
    3. Are there any funders/commissioners involved and if so the nature of their role?
